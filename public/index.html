Hilary Parker

Hilary Parker is a Data Scientist at Stitch Fix and co-founder of the Not So Standard Deviations podcast. She focuses on R, experimentation, and rigorous analysis development methods such as reproducibility. Formerly a Senior Data Analyst at Etsy, she received a PhD in Biostatistics from the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health.
Jennifer Bryan
Associate Professor
University of British Columbia
Jennifer Bryan is Associate Professor of Statistics at the University of British Columbia and the Academic Director of UBC's new Master of Data Science program. Her work is focused on creating workflows and tools for the full cycle of data analysis, from ingest to communication, with an emphasis on openness and reproducibility. She shares teaching material at STAT545.com and serves in the leadership of rOpenSci.

satRdays are community-led, one-day, regional conferences inspired by SQLSaturday.

satRday in Cape Town

A satRday will be held in Cape Town on 18 February 2017. Both Registration and Call for Papers are open.

The goals of Cape Town satRday are to

  • provide an affordable learning experience for R users;
  • support collaboration, networking and innovation within the R community;
  • demonstrate the need for such regional conferences.

If you want to get in touch with the satRday Cape Town organising committee, please feel free to mail us.

Pre-Conference Workshops

We'll be hosting workshops on the two days prior to satRday (16 and 17 February 2017).

  • Introduction to A/B Testing (Hilary Parker)
  • Using Git and GitHub with R, RStudio, and R Markdown (Jennifer Bryan)
  • Shiny Workshop (Julia Silge)

Conference Programme

The satRday programme will be a full day of R goodness, including submitted talks, posters, short workshops and keynote talks.

These are the keynote talks we've lined up:

  • Opinionated Analysis Development (Hilary Parker)
  • Behind every great plot there's a great deal of wrangling (Jennifer Bryan)
  • Text Mining with Tidy Tools (Julia Silge)

satRday in Budapest

The first satRday was held in Budapest, Hungary, on 3 September 2016. There were two keynote presentations, six workshops and twenty contributed talks. We envisage a similar programme for the Cape Town satRday.

Call for Papers

Presentation proposals for satRday Cape Town are now open.

What are the benefits of giving a presentation?

  • speakers will have their registration fees refunded;
  • financial support may be possible for speakers;
  • get prompt feedback on your proposed presentation; and
  • have the opportunity to speak at the first satRday in Africa (and the second in the World!).

Feel free to submit one or more proposal(s) at the above URL. We will be considering the following presentation formats:

  • Workshop [90 min]
  • Lightning Talk [5 min]
  • Standard Talk [20 min]
  • Poster
